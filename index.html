Books that have Inspired Me Lately

The following are books of prose I've read in the last six months that have inspired me as a writer.

World of Wonders

Aimee Nezhukumatathil

2020

Nonfiction

Set of memoiristic nature essays

Masterfully written, carries a powerful message. Makes me want to write memoiristic nature essays. Beautiful both inside and out.

Leavetakings

Corinna Cook

2020

Nonfiction

Memoiristic Nature Essays

Set in Southeast Alaska, where I grew up in the summers. Reminds me of home, and inspires me so much.

Why We Dream

Alice Robb

2018

Nonfiction

Dream Research Presentation and Discussion

Incredible, eye-opening view into our brains during night. Confirms what I've always believed, that dreams are so imcredible important.

The Cassandra

Sharma Shields

2019

Fiction

Historical Literary Thriller

Amazing and disturbing surreal imagery in her writing. Complex characters. Carries an important message.

Sins of the Bees

Annie Lampman

2020

Fiction

Contemporary Literary Thriller

Lyrical writing, complex characters, and great tension and pacing.

The Secret History

Donna Tartt

1992

Fiction

Contemporary Literary Mystery

Complex characters and philosophies that start on the page and yet somehow become real.

The Girl Who Loved Tom Gordon

Stephen King

1999

Fiction

Contemporary Literary Thriller

Incredible writing (duh, look at the author), captivating descriptions and character arc.

Shards of Memory

Ruth Prawler Jhabvala

1995

Fiction

Generational Literary Fiction

Odd in its glory. Descriptive writing, and unique characters that are so odd they seem to be real.

After the Quake

Haruki Murakami

2000

Fiction

Contemporary Literary Short Stories

Strange, beautiful, surreal imagery. Each short story is connected by a thread, yet independent in their own ways.
